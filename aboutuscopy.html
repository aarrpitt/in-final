  • Who are we?

    Indra Water (Inphlox Water Systems Pvt Ltd.) is an Indian company located in Mumbai, Maharashtra which is pioneering the decentralized treatment of wastewater with patented electrically driven modular systems at the point of the source recovering up to 99% of the water for reuse applications. Our physioelectrical treatment technology is based on an industry-first approach to mass production of water plants driven by standardization, data analytics, smart software and firmware and our novel structural reactor.

    Back in 2017, two batchmates, Amrit Om Nayak and Krunal Patel, from Seattle’s University of Washington, decided to give wings to their ideas and projects conceived during their Master’s programme. They developed a small model to treat stormwater and then made it reusable for landscaping and gardening. During that point, they realised the technology might also be used to treat effluent water and other waste streams. At a boiler facility in the US, they conducted another pilot. The solution worked effectively on the boiler waste too. It made them believe they were holding onto a valid solution. After the pilots were successful, Krunal and Amrit set out to build a prototype for the sewage and wastewater treatment system. They tested the prototype at Mumbai's KJ Somaiya College after returning to India in 2017. Later, Abhijit VVR an alumnus of BITS Pilani also joined them as one of the co-founders.

    Indra won the urban water challenge at World Water Week 2019 in Stockholm, from among 236 companies across 38 countries, and also won the Millenium Alliance award. Presently, we are incubated at SINE IIT Bombay, and Riidl and have about 6 million litres per day capacity engagements with major industries and corporates across India and are also backed by Imagine H2O (International Water Accelerator) for expanding to Southeast Asian markets.
